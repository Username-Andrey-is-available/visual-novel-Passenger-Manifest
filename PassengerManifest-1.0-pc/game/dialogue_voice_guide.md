# Dialogue Voice Guide

## Evan Carter
- Confidence: medium-high in analysis, lower in action under pressure.
- Control vs trust: tries to control through structure; trusts reluctantly, then deeply.
- Logic vs emotion: logic first, emotion leaks through fatigue and care.
- Tone: professional, reflective, concise when focused.
- Secrecy vs directness: careful with others, brutally honest in private thought.
- Speech markers: short conclusions, self-corrections, dry observations, occasional dark irony.

## Lena Brooks
- Confidence: high when the subject is technical, shaky only when out of depth.
- Control vs trust: prefers control through tools and process; trust must be earned.
- Logic vs emotion: strongly logic-driven, but fear appears as irritation.
- Tone: practical, clipped, workshop-direct, lightly sarcastic.
- Secrecy vs directness: direct under pressure, hides vulnerability behind work.
- Speech markers: short technical phrases, "сейчас", "не тяну", "поехали", dry humor.

## Marcus Reed
- Confidence: high, especially in risk assessment and containment.
- Control vs trust: strongly control-oriented, low default trust.
- Logic vs emotion: logic framed through threat and consequences.
- Tone: professional, terse, security-minded.
- Secrecy vs directness: guarded, but speaks plainly once a decision is made.
- Speech markers: measured warnings, procedural language, short hard stops.

## Kenji Tanaka
- Confidence: calm and steady, rarely performs certainty.
- Control vs trust: moderate control, above-average trust when he sees sincerity.
- Logic vs emotion: balanced; speaks through human cost and clinical logic together.
- Tone: professional, gentle, human.
- Secrecy vs directness: tactful, but not evasive.
- Speech markers: calm framing, metaphor through medicine, quiet reassurance.

## Sarah Coleman
- Confidence: high and socially agile.
- Control vs trust: trusts conditionally, prefers influence over control.
- Logic vs emotion: balanced, but packages logic in vivid human language.
- Tone: conversational, journalistic, quick, lightly ironic.
- Secrecy vs directness: usually direct, but can pivot when probing.
- Speech markers: punchy lines, wit, concrete images, sharp emotional read.

## Thomas Gallagher
- Confidence: uneven in life, solid when the subject is real piloting.
- Control vs trust: distrustful of systems, more trust in people who prove nerve.
- Logic vs emotion: practical instinct over theory; emotion comes through old scars.
- Tone: conversational, veteran, rough-edged.
- Secrecy vs directness: direct, sometimes blunt.
- Speech markers: old-school phrasing, pilot slang flavor, anti-bureaucratic grumbling.

## Captain Daniel Hayes
- Confidence: very high outwardly, never allows visible drift.
- Control vs trust: maximal control, trust only with proof or necessity.
- Logic vs emotion: logic and command discipline first.
- Tone: formal, clipped, commanding.
- Secrecy vs directness: secretive by default, direct when issuing decisions.
- Speech markers: command verbs, compressed phrasing, no wasted softness.

## Mira Shaw
- Confidence: outwardly quiet, inwardly absolute.
- Control vs trust: low visible control, but constantly steers the frame of the conversation.
- Logic vs emotion: abstract logic with emotional undertow.
- Tone: soft, precise, unsettlingly calm.
- Secrecy vs directness: highly indirect until cornered.
- Speech markers: oblique phrasing, layered meaning, questions instead of declarations, eerie calm.
